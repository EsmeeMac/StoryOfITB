ITB's Health Servce

ITB have a Nurse on campus five days a week. No appoitment is needed. You can book free doctor appointments through her.
ITB's Nurse is Margaret Rushe, she trained in Letterkenny, worked in Saint Vincents with Cystic Fibrosis patients, has worked in Australia and in all the major hospitals across Dublin.
Margaret Rushe has worked in ITB for two and a half years. Prior to her there was a nurse who has worked in ITB since 1999.
ITB have always offered a free GP service. A Doctor is on campus on Tuesday and Thursday from 9:30am to 12:30pm

The National Learning Network

The National Learning Network (NLN) have been in ITB since 2003. They are situated in the A block.
They are a psychology based education service. They help student with mental and physical disabilities and depression or anyone who needs help with college work. It is a drop in service and everyone is welcome.
They take a holistic approach and understand that your academic achievements are intertwined with your well being. They give students acdemic support, well being support and social support.
Roisin Long is the Assistant Psychologist, she has worked in ITB since March and had a Masters in Psychology from UCD. She along with the other psychologists offer a one to one service to students who are struggling with the workload and essay writing. They also offer study skills sessions to groups. The first semester is always very busy for them, they see on average 20 student a week.
There are two assistant psychologists, two educational psychologists who help students who need a dyslexia assesments, a peer mentor and the adminastrator.
