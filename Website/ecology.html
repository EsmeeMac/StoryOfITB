Our Aim

At ITB there is a wide range of biodiversity. ITB aim to protect and respect the enviroment our college surrounds.

Plant Life

ITB have planted many trees around campus, many trees are less than 20 years old.
Along the border of campus there are many old Ash tree's along with a natural drainage system that is a few hundread years old. The Ash tree dominates the other trees on campus.

The hedges that surround ITB have been around since 1837.

Farming has effected the ecology around ITB. Over the last 40 years farming has intensified to meet production demands. As a result most of the wildflower-rich meadows have been lost resulting in less support for wildlife.

Wildlife

The Bramble Shrubs support foxes and many species of birds. The grass around campus is perfect length for rabbits to live. There is evidence of rabbit holes around the hedgerows.

A man-made pond was built to support wildlife. The pond is nutrient rich and acts as a floodplain.
Swans, ducks and herons can be seen at the pond along with many more birds.

Is ITB enviromentally healthy?

Short answer, yes it is. There is lichen around campus which is a sign of healthy clean air.
